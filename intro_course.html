Welcome to Introduction to Cyber Security

Learn about common cyber threats and key cyber security terms.

What is Cyber Security?

Definition: Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks.

The Aim of Cyber Security:

  • Confidentiality - Keeping personal information private.
  • Integrity - Ensuring data is accurate and not modified.
  • Availability - Making sure services and data are accessible when required.

Why is Cyber Security Important?

We live in a digital age where everyone is vulnerable to cyber attacks. Cyber security protects your personal information, prevents identity theft, and avoids financial loss.

Common Cyber Threats

Malware

Malware, short for ‘malicious software,’ includes harmful software like viruses, trojans, and spyware. It can steal information or cause damage.

Types of Malware:

  • Viruses - Attach themselves to legitimate files and spread.
  • Trojans - Disguised as safe programs but can harm your device.
  • Spyware - Secretly records your activity, like keystrokes.

How to Prevent Malware Attacks:

  • Avoid opening email attachments from unknown sources.
  • Install and update antivirus software.
  • Be cautious on suspicious websites.

Key Cyber Security Terms

  • Encryption - Protects data by scrambling it, readable only with the correct key.
  • Antivirus Software - Detects, blocks, and removes malware.
  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) - Adds a second layer of security for account access.
