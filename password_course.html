Welcome to Password Management

Learn how to create and manage strong passwords to protect your accounts.

What is a password?

Definition: A password is a combination of letters, numbers and special characters which are used to protect your online accounts.

Why are passwords important?

If you have a weak password, it can be easily guessed or cracked which allows attackers to access your personal information. Strong passwords make it more difficult for attackers to break into your accounts - keeping your personal information safe.

Consequences of weak passwords

  • Unauthorised access to your online accounts.
  • Stolen personal information.
  • Comprised banking data which can lead to financial loss

Strong password creation

Qualities of a strong password

  • 1. Length- between 12 to 16 characters
  • 2. Complexity - a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers and special characters such as !, @, Â£, #, %
  • 3. Unpredictability - common words or personal information such as name or birth year
  • Uniqueness - different passwords for each of your accounts

Example of weak password:

John123

Example of strong password:

B3!red@2

Tips for strong passwords

  • Use words which are unrelated in order to create a phrase e.g. $Fish!Blue22.
  • Replace letters with numbers or special characters e.g. Pa33w0rd!
  • Avoid repeating characters

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

What is two-factor authentication (2FA)?

2FA adds extra security to online accounts as it needs two forms of verification before you can access your account. It requires your passwords and a second factor for example a code is sent to your phone or an app generated code.

Types of 2FA

  • SMS Code - one time code received by text message.
  • Authenticator app - an app such as Google Authenticator to generate a time-based code
  • Biometrics - use facial recognition or fingerprint

Why use 2FA?

By using 2FA even if someone has your password, they will not be able to gain access to your account without the second factor.
