January 2025

In January 2025, tAs we enter the new year, cybercriminals continue to target individuals, particularly seniors, with new scams and online threats. Staying informed and taking precautions can help protect you from fraud. Here are the latest cyber threats for January 2025.

New Year Financial Scams

With many people reviewing their finances in January, scammers are posing as financial advisors, banks, and investment firms offering ‘exclusive’ financial opportunities. These scams may come through emails, phone calls, or fake websites claiming to help you save money or invest. Be cautious of unsolicited financial advice.

Fake Health & Fitness Subscription

January is a popular time for fitness new years resolutions and scammers are taking advantages by sending fake emails offering free trails for gym memberships, diet plans or wellness apps. Always sign up for subscriptions through official websites.

Fake Energy Bill Discounts

Winter energy costs are still high, scammers are sending emails and text messages claiming to offer government backed energy discounts. These scams request personal details or small processing fee to claim the benefits. Vist the government website to check for legitmate schemes.
