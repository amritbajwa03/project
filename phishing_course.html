Phishing Awareness

Phishing is a common type of cyber attack where attackers try to trick you into giving away personal information like passwords, bank details, or credit card numbers. These attacks are often carried out through fake emails, messages, or phone calls that appear legitimate.

How to Identify a Phishing Attempt

  • Suspicious Sender: Check if the sender's email address looks strange or unfamiliar.
  • Urgent or Scary Messages: Be cautious of messages that create a sense of urgency, such as "Your account will be locked" or "Immediate action required!"
  • Links and Attachments: Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
  • Spelling and Grammar Mistakes: Phishing messages often contain spelling errors or poor grammar.

Examples of Common Phishing Scams

  • Banking Scams: Fake emails claiming to be from your bank, asking you to verify your account.
  • Delivery Scams: Messages from fake delivery services asking for additional payment or confirmation of your details.
  • Tech Support Scams: Calls or emails claiming your computer has a virus and asking for remote access.
  • Charity Scams: Fake charity requests asking for donations, especially during crises.

What to Do If You Receive a Suspicious Message

  • Do not click on any links or download attachments.
  • Verify the sender's identity by contacting the organisation directly using a trusted phone number or website.
  • Delete the message if you are unsure.
  • Report phishing emails to your email provider or relevant authorities.

Protecting Yourself from Phishing

  • Use a strong and unique password for each account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
  • Keep your computer and software up to date.
  • Be cautious when sharing personal information online.
