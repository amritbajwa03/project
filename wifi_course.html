Welcome to Staying Safe on Public Wi-Fi

Learn how to use public Wi-Fi safely and protect your data.

Understanding the Risks of Public Wi-Fi

What is Public Wi-Fi?

Public Wi-Fi is wireless internet networks available in public areas. Public Wi-Fi is open to everyone which makes it more vulnerable to security threats.

Dangers of Public Wi-Fi

  • Unencrypted Connections: Many public Wi-Fi networks do not use encryption, this means your data can be intercepted.
  • Man-in-the-Middle Attacks: Hackers can position themselves between you and the Wi-Fi network to steal your data.
  • Fake Wi-Fi Hotspots: Cybercriminals create fake networks with familiar names (e.g., "Free Airport Wi-Fi") to lure users into connecting.
  • Session Hijacking: Attackers can steal cookies from your browsing session and gain access to your accounts.
  • Data Snooping: Hackers can monitor and record what you are doing online.

Best Practices for Public Wi-Fi

Always ask staff for the correct Wi-Fi name before connecting.

Avoid accessing sensitive accounts such as online banking, email, and social media.

Look for HTTPS websites when entering personal details; the “S” means secure encryption.

Turn off automatic Wi-Fi connections in your device settings to prevent unwanted connections.

Forget the network after using public Wi-Fi to avoid reconnecting automatically.

Use a Virtual Private Network (VPN)

A VPN encrypts your internet traffic, which makes it difficult for hackers to intercept your data.

If you frequently use public Wi-Fi, consider using a VPN for extra security.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an extra layer of security to your online accounts as it requires an additional verification step.

This means that even if a hacker gains access to your password, they won’t be able to log in without the second authentication step.

Additional Security Measures

Adjust Your Device Settings

  • Disable File Sharing: Turn off file sharing in your device’s settings to prevent unwanted access.
  • Use a Firewall: Ensure your firewall is enabled so that malicious traffic is blocked.
  • Software Updated Always update your operating system and apps to fix security vulnerabilities.

Use Your Mobile Data Instead

  • If you need to access sensitive accounts, use your mobile network (4G/5G) instead of public Wi-Fi. Mobile networks are generally more secure.

Turn Off Bluetooth and Wi-Fi When Not in Use

  • Hackers can exploit Bluetooth and Wi-Fi connections to access your device.
  • Disable these features when you don’t need them.

Recognising Public Wi-Fi Scams

Signs of Fake Public Wi-Fi

  • Unusual Network Names: If you see multiple similar networks, avoid them.
  • No Password Required: A legitimate Wi-Fi network usually requires a password.
  • Pop-ups Asking for Personal Information: Never enter personal details to access free Wi-Fi.

What to Do If You Suspect a Public Wi-Fi Scam

  • Disconnect immediately if you feel something is suspicious.
  • Avoid entering login credentials or payment details.
  • Report suspicious networks to the business providing the Wi-Fi.
