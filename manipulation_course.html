What is Manipulation in Cyber Security?

Definition: Manipulation in cyber security is the tactics attackers use to deceive individuals into revealing sensitive information, taking harmful actions, or giving access to protected systems. Attackers often exploit human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

Types of manipulation used in cyber attacks:

  • Psychological manipulation - Exploiting emotions such as fear, trust, or urgency.
  • Technical manipulation - Using deceptive techniques like fake websites or malware.

Example

An attacker sends an email pretending to be from a bank, asking the victim to verify their account details by clicking on a malicious link.

Impersonation and Pretexting

  • Impersonation: Attackers pretend to be trusted figures to gain access to information.

    • Example: IT support scams, CEO fraud, law enforcement scams.

  • Pretexting: Creating a fake scenario to obtain information.

    • Example: An attacker calls a helpdesk pretending to be an employee who lost access to their account.

Fear, Urgency, and Authority

Fear and urgency are used to manipulate victims into making rushed decisions.

For Example:

  • “Your account will be deactivated in 24 hours unless you verify your details.”
  • “You owe unpaid taxes! Pay immediately to avoid arrest.”

Reciprocity, Trust, and Guilt

  • Reciprocity: Offering fake free trials, discounts, or giveaways to gain trust.
  • Trust: Attackers use familiar brands, people, or fake testimonials.
  • Guilt: Fake charities manipulating victims into donating.

Malicious Links and Fake Websites

Attackers use spoofed websites that look identical to real ones to steal login credentials.

  • Typosquatting: Registering domains like "paypa1.com" instead of "paypal.com".
  • Homoglyph attacks: Using similar looking letters, e.g., “g00gle.com” instead of “google.com”.

Deepfakes, AI Manipulation, and Social Engineering

Attackers are now using AI-generated deepfake videos and voice cloning to impersonate executives, politicians, and even family members.

How Hackers Use Social Media for Attacks

Attackers harvest personal data from platforms like Facebook and LinkedIn to make scams more convincing.

  • Public profiles make spear phishing easier.
  • Photos of work ID badges can be exploited.
