October 2024

In October 2024, there were several cyber threats which targeted individuals in the UK. Having awareness of these scams is important in order to protect yourself.

Fake DVLA Emails

Scammers sent emails pretending to be the DVLA claiming that vehicle tax payments had failed. These emails included links which steal personal and financial information. DVLA and National Cyber Security Centre have emphasised that they will never request bank details via email or text message. If you do receive an email, do not click on any links and report it to report@phishing.gov.uk.

Fake Driving Instructors

Individuals are posing as driving instructors or schools, offering services and collecting payment information but not providing any lessons. This scam takes advantage of the high demand for driving tests. Make sure to always verify the credentials of the driving instructor before making any payments.

Winter Fuel Payments

Scammers sent out text messages which claimed to offer winter fuel payments from the UK government. The aim of these text messages is to steal personal and financial information. The government would never send this type of text message. If you receive any suspicious texts, do not respond or click any links. Forward the message to 7726 to report it.
